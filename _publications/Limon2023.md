Using a hierarchy of simplified configurations within NCAR's Community Atmosphere Model (CAM6), we demonstrate that while random forest emulators show strong skill in reproducing physical parameterizations, their performance degrades systematically as model complexity increases, suggesting fundamental limitations on their feasibility as physics emulators in state-of-the-art climate models.
