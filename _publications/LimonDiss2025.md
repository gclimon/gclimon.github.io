This dissertation examines the integration of machine learning into climate and weather modeling across three fronts: emulating physical processes in the Community Atmosphere Model, coupling ML components into live climate simulations, and developing dynamical evaluation frameworks for AI-driven forecasting systems like Google's GraphCast. Together, these studies identify key scientific and engineering barriers to responsible ML deployment in Earth system models while charting paths toward more physically interpretable, rigorously tested approaches.
